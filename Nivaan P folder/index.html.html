Dopamine & Modern Addiction

üí≠ Addiction...this word is deeper than you think. When one thinks about addiction, the first thing that comes to mind is usually substance abuse, however it wears many faces.

üì± All the way from smartphones to gambling, addiction encompasses countless matters.

‚ö° However, the factor, the trigger, the element that causes the feeling of addiction is none other than dopamine. Dopamine is basically a brain chemical that helps control reward, learning and motivation. Neurons in the brain release dopamine when one does an activity that is pleasurable and rewarding.

üîÑ When it is released, it signals to the brain that "this action gave a reward, repeat it".

Dopamine & The Brain

Dopamine travels between neurons using neural pathways. It affects learning, memory, movement, and emotional responses. Balanced dopamine levels help maintain mental well-being.

DANGERS OF ARTIFICIAL DOPAMINE SOURCES

Our research focuses on preventing dopamine intake from unhealthy sources. These include:

  • Excessive social media and screen time
  • Gambling and risk-seeking behaviors
  • Substance abuse (drugs, alcohol)
  • Unhealthy eating habits (sugar addiction)
  • Unregulated supplements and medications

Why avoid artificial dopamine boosters?

1. Addiction Risk: Artificial sources can create dependency

2. Tolerance Build-up: You need more to get the same effect

3. Natural Balance Disruption: Your brain's natural regulation gets impaired

4. Withdrawal Symptoms: Stopping can cause depression and anxiety

Our Message: Focus on natural, sustainable ways to maintain dopamine balance!
